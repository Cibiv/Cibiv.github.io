DupliDeli - Estimation of Gene Family Specific Duplication and Deletion Rates

Introduction:

DupliDeli is a computer program for the estimation of gene family specific duplication and deletion rates as well as the ancestral number of gene copies. It uses a stochastic birth and death process to model the change in gene family size along a phylogenetic tree. For the specific numbers of gene family members of extant species, and given the corresponding species tree for these species, DupliDeli can estimate the birth and death rate specific for the gene family. DupliDeli is written in the object-oriented programming language Java.

Reference:

For for details on the method refer to:
  • Andrea Fuehrer (2007) Maximum likelihood estimation of Gene family specific duplication and deletion rates. PhD Thesis, University of Vienna, Austria.
